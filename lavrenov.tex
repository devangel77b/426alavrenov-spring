\begin{abstract}
Matrix New World Engineering (MNWE) is an environmental engineering firm, a sub-discipline of civil engineering, owned by True Environmental, whose goal is to use engineering to reform local and national infrastructure challenges. Over the course of a few months, I spent my time revising small projects, including reviewing specifications for a utility vault and performing quantity takeoffs for a site plan, which helped me understand the workflow and documentation practices required of an engineer before I received my workstation. A large component of this internship involved plan revision checking, where I would compare two plot sheets (one with the initial plan, the other with revisions visible in a distinct color), verifying that all updates had been accurately incorporated. Through researching one of the site plans for a municipality requiring traffic control modifications, I had to consider MUTCD sign standards, specifically nominal sizing and codes. After transferring to a proper workstation, this internship started involving Autodesk AutoCAD Civil 3D, beginning with a project where I reconstructed a shed from a published plan into the CAD software, learning how to adapt to explicit modeling from a parametric modeling software, Autodesk Inventor, which I was more familiar with. Building on this foundation, I began two main projects: the first involved working on the Paterson STEAM School civil plan set, this time directly updating the plots myself, requiring me to learn AutoCAD Layers and suppress or unsuppress them depending on project specifications; the second, Fort Mott State Park's septic systems, required research on SESC (Soil Erosion and Sediment Control) plans due to the proximity of adjacent wetlands. My final project was to analyze 100 residential floating docks in the New York City area, noting the shape, number of piles, area, and longest measurement of each structure using Nearmap's aerial and oblique views, producing a structured dataset to establish baseline pile requirements relative to dock size and geometry. 
\end{abstract}
